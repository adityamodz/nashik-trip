The Trailer Of Vaikunta!

Hare Krishna!

On the morning of April 3rd, at 6 o'clock, everyone gathered at Kalyan railway station, near ticket counter platform number 1. We proceeded to platform number 4. Our train arrived at 6:35 am, 10 minutes delayed. Once we were seated, our journey from Kalyan to Nashik began at 6:40 am. We had our breakfast of Medu Vada and Idli Chutney on the train. We reached Nashik at 9:20 in the morning and boarded a bus where HG Baldev Prabhu ji sang beautiful Kirtan while we were travelling. At 9:40 am, we arrived at our hotel and rested for a while.

At 10:40 am, HG Krishnagaur Prabhu ji & HG Baldev Prabhu ji gave us an orientation lecture where he explained the do's and don'ts for the trip and the value of our journey. We departed for the ISKCON temple at 11:45 am, and within 5-6 minutes by foot, we arrived while singing Kirtan. As soon as we reached the temple, we started a wonderful session of Bhajan and Kirtan. We danced and had a darshan of the beautiful idols of Krishna ji, Radha ji and their Gopis. The view was spectacular and mesmerized our hearts. The session continued until 12:45 pm, after which we had a sumptuous feast for lunch. We ate Chapati with Kadhi Pakoda, Rice with Sambar, and as a complementary dish, Kanda Bhaji and Jalebi for dessert.

After such an amazing lunch, we gathered upstairs at the terrace at around 3:30 pm for a session of Sangit Mehfil. Various activities were performed by our classmates. Peeyusha danced, followed by a group drama played by Peeyusha, Aryan Pathak, Samarth, Sashank, Tanmay Mishra, Parth Rajput, Pramod and Aum. The drama based on *Incident of chaitnya mahaprabhuu's life that tells us importance of bhakti and krishna consciousness through story of jagai madai. In the end, Peeyusha and Shraddha danced. After all the performances, HG Krishnagaur Prabhu ji gave us our regular spiritual lecture(held every Monday), which lasted from 3:55 pm until 5:00 pm. We then played the game of Dam Shararat, with a twist to enact the historical characters of Mahabharat and Ramayana. Four teams were formed, and Team Ram emerged as the winner, while Team Lakshman was in the last place.

From 6:30 to 8:00 pm, we had another Bhajan and Kirtan session (Sandhya Bhajan & Kirtan), and at 8:30 pm, we had dinner, which included Khichdi and Fryams. We reached our hotels by 9:30 pm, and before going to sleep, we were all called for a small meeting at 9:45 pm to explain the plan for the next day (April 4th) and to celebrate Peeyusha's birthday. Prabhu ji instructed us to wake up at 4 am as Mangal Arati darshan would begin at 4:30 am. We traditionally celebrated Peeyusha's birthday, and Prabhu Ji arranged Radha Krishna's Mala for her. After traditionally singing Happy Birthday, we enjoyed cake, and everyone went to their designated rooms. Some people stayed up all night, while others slept.

On the following day, at the crack of dawn, we were all set to embark on a spiritual journey that had been long-awaited. However, due to tardiness on the part of some, we were a bit behind schedule, and by the time we departed, it was already 4:45 am. Our destination was ISCON, where we were slated to witness the Mangal arati darshan at 4:30 am, so we had to make haste.

As we made our way to the temple, we engaged in bead chanting at 6 am, which continued until 7:00 am when our college mates joined us. After wonderful breakfast consisting of poha, medu vada & sambar, and lemonade, we indulged in a soulful bhajan and Kirtan session until 8:30 am, when our cars arrived. Then, we set out on a local spiritual trip to visit various holy places and temples in and around Nashik.

Our first stop was the Ram Kund temple, followed by a visit to the Tapovan River. There, we got to know the history and stories of the Tapovan River through Ram sukh Prabhu ji, the rahavaasee of Nashik Dham, who enlightened us about the Tapovan River and its significance. From there, we proceeded to Sita Gufa, a site renowned for its association with the kidnapping of Sita by Ravana, the king of Lanka.

After the visit, we had a soda party that lifted our spirits. Then, we moved on to the Shree Kalaram Sansthan Mandir, where Baldev Prabhu ji sang some mellifluous kirtan that brought us and the strangers present together. Our next destination was the Shree Kapaleshwar Mahadev Mandir, where we had the darshan of Lord Mahadev. Finally, we reached the Godavari River, where we bathed before heading to an ice cream parlour for a Kulfi party.

At around 2:30 pm, we reached ISCON Temple for lunch, which consisted of chapati, paneer bhaji, rice & dal. For dessert, we were served a scrumptious semiya kheer. After lunch, we returned to the hotel and rested until 4:30-5 pm, when we gathered to check out. By 5:00 pm, we had reached the station, and our train was scheduled to leave from platform no. 3 at 6:02 pm. However, as is often the case, the train was delayed and arrived at 6:15 pm.

Once we boarded the train, we settled into our seats and enjoyed some delicious samosas for snacks during the journey. Finally, at 8:45 pm, we reached Kalyan Station, and the trip came to a successful end. We attributed the success of the trip to the grace of Lord Krishna, and we hoped for more such spiritual journeys in the future.

Hare Krishna.
